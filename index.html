Mathematical GST Formulas

To add GST to a base amount (exclusive calculation):

GST Amount = Original Cost × (GST Rate / 100)
Total Price = Original Cost + GST Amount

To separate/remove GST from an all-inclusive price (inclusive calculation):

Original Base Cost = Total Price / (1 + (GST Rate / 100))
GST Tax Amount = Total Price - Original Base Cost

The type of tax applied depends on the place of supply:

  • Intra-state transactions: Tax is split 50/50 as Central GST (CGST) and State GST (SGST).
  • Inter-state transactions: 100% Integrated GST (IGST) is applied centrally.

Cost-Plus Pricing & Markup Margins

The cost-plus pricing calculator helps retail and manufacturing businesses determine the optimal selling price for products by factoring in materials, labor, overheads, and target markup percentages.

Total Cost = Material Cost + Labor Cost + Overhead Cost
Markup Amount = Total Cost × (Markup Rate / 100)
Selling Price = Total Cost + Markup Amount
Profit Margin (%) = (Markup Rate / (100 + Markup Rate)) × 100

Startup Cash Runway & Burn Rate

For tech startups, MSMEs, and early-stage companies, managing cash runway is crucial for survival. Calculate your runway in months and simulate the impacts of funding or cost reductions.

Net Monthly Burn = Monthly Expenses - Monthly Revenue
Runway (Months) = Current Cash Balance / Net Monthly Burn

Working Capital Gap & Operating Cycle

Understand your business liquidity and cash conversion cycle. Calculate the operational cash flow gap between paying suppliers and receiving payments from clients.

Working Capital Gap = (Accounts Receivable + Inventory) - Accounts Payable
Cash Conversion Cycle (Days) = Days Inventory Outstanding + Days Sales Outstanding - Days Payable Outstanding

Razorpay Fee & Net Payout Calculations

Plan your sales, billing, and freelancing project payouts securely. Calculate Razorpay processing fees (standard 2% on credit cards/wallets, 3% on international credit cards) and account for the mandatory 18% GST on the processing fee to see exactly how much cash settles in your bank.

Gateway Fee = Invoice Amount × (Processor Rate / 100)
GST on Fee = Gateway Fee × 0.18
Total Deductions = Gateway Fee + GST on Fee
Net Payout = Invoice Amount - Total Deductions
